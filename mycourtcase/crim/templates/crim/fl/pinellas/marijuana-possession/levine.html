Marijuana Possession Cases in Judge Levine's Courtroom

37% of people charged with Marijuana Possession are convicted. 6% of cases are dismissed, and 56% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record. In Marijuana Possession cases getting a withhold of adjudication is especially important because a conviction carries with it a one-year driver's license suspension.

Do people hire an attorney for Marijuana Possession cases in Judge Levine's courtroom?

18% of people charged with Marijuana Possession hire a private attorney in Judge Levine's courtroom. 32% go with the Public Defender, while 50% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases in Judge Levine's courtroom. People who hired a private attorney were convicted just 26% of the time, while people who had the public defender were convicted 61% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," were convicted 27% of the time.

Across Pinellas County people who hired a private attorney are convicted of Marijuana Possession 29% of the time, while people represented by the public defender are convicted 62% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own, also known as "Pro Se", are convicted 28% of the time.

Can I get my Marijuana Possession case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 12% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 3% of the time. People without an attorney have their case dismissed 6% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hired a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 62% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 36% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 68% of the time.

Remember that getting a withhold of adjudication instead of a conviction is especially important in Marijuana Possession cases, because a conviction carries with it a one-year license suspension.

What is the typical penalty for Marijuana Possession?

The most common penalty for Marijuana Possession is six months probation, community service, a drug class, and a fine in the range of $200-300.
