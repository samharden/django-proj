Marijuana Possession Cases in Pinellas County

40% of people charged with Marijuana Possession are convicted. 8% of cases are dismissed, and 52% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record. In Marijuana Possession cases getting a withhold of adjudication is especially important because a conviction carries with it a one-year driver's license suspension.

Do people hire an attorney for Marijuana Possession cases in Pinellas County?

19% of people charged with Marijuana Possession hire a private attorney in Pinellas county. 34% go with the Public Defender, while 47% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage over the public defender in these cases. People who hired a private attorney were convicted just 29% of the time, while people who had the public defender were convicted 62% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," were convicted 28% of the time.

Can I get my Marijuana Possession case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their cases dismissed 13% of the time, while people who use the public defender have their cases dismissed 5% of the time. People without an attorney, also known as "Pro Se," have their case dismissed 9% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hired a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 58% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 33% of the time. People without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 63% of the time.

Remember that getting a withhold of adjudication instead of a conviction is especially important in Marijuana Possession cases, because a conviction carries with it a one-year license suspension.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

People with cases in front of Judges Overton and Pierce are convicted of Marijuana Possession 62% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judges Bedinghaus and Carballo are convicted only 36% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judge Horrox have their cases dismissed 10% of the time, while no one with a Marijuana Possession case in Judge Pierce's courtroom had their case dismissed.

People with cases in front of Judge Bedinghaus receive a withhold of adjudication 59% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge Pierce receive a withhold of adjudication 36% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Marijuana Possession?

The most common penalty for Marijuana Possession is six months probation, community service, a drug class, and a fine in the range of $200-300.
