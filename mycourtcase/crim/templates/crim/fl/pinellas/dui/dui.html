DUI Cases in Pinellas County

86% of DUI cases in Pinellas County result in a DUI conviction. 10% of DUI cases are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving. Only 4% of people avoid a DUI conviction by having their case dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Pinellas County?

Yes. More people hire an attorney for a DUI charge than for any other misdemeanor traffic crime in Pinellas county. 52% of people charged with a DUI hire a private attorney. 36% are represented by the Public Defender. 11% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Yes. People who hire a private attorney avoid a DUI conviction 17% of the time, while people who had the public defender avoid a DUI conviction 10% of the time. People who do not have an attorney and represent themselves ("Pro Se") only avoid a DUI conviction 7% of the time.

People who hire a private attorney are convicted of DUI in 83% of cases. People who use the public defender are convicted of DUI 90% of the time. People who go without an attorney and represent themselves ("Pro Se") are convicted of DUI 93% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

The average rate of conviction for DUI cases across all judges is 87%. People with private attorneys are convicted less often than average in most  judge's courtrooms.

People with the public defender are convicted more often than the average in all judge’s courtrooms in Pinellas County.

People who go without an attorney and represent themselves are convicted more often than the average in nearly every judge’s courtroom.
