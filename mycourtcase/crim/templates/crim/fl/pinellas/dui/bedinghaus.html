DUI Cases in Judge Bedinghaus' Courtroom

In Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom 74% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 20% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 6% of DUI cases are dismissed or dropped.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom?

Nearly everyone in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom who is accused of DUI has an attorney for their case. The majority of people (65%) hire a private attorney, while 31% use the public defender. Only 4% of people do not hire an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is an advantage in DUI cases in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction, either through having their case dismissed or reduced to reckless driving, 32% of the time, while people who had the public defender only avoided a DUI conviction 12% of the time. People who went Pro Se avoided a DUI conviction 15% of the time.

The average for judges in Pinellas County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and slightly lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 17% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 10% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 7% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 6% of DUI cases in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom are dismissed or dropped.
People who hire a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 6% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 4% of the time, while 15% of people went without an attorney had their DUI case dismissed or dropped.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

20% of DUI cases in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving.
People who hire a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 27% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 8% of the time. No one who went without an attorney had their DUI case reduced to Reckless Driving.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

Over the past five years only 40 people represented themselves in a DUI case in Judge Bedinghaus' courtroom. They were convicted at a much higher rate than people who hired a private attorney.
