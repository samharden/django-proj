DUI Cases in Judge Pierce's Courtroom

In Judge Pierce's courtroom 76% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 20% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 4% of DUI cases are dismissed or dropped.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Pierce's courtroom?

85% of people in Judge Pierce's courtroom who is accused of DUI have an attorney for their case. 64% of people hire a private attorney, while 21% use the public defender. Only 15% of people do not hire an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is an advantage in DUI cases in Judge Pierce's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction, either through having their case dismissed or reduced to reckless driving, 30% of the time, while people who had the public defender avoided a DUI conviction 17% of the time. 10% of people without an attorney avoided a DUI conviction.

The average for judges in Pinellas County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and higher for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 17% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 10% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 7% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 4% of DUI cases in Judge Pierce's courtroom are dismissed.
People who hired a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 4% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 5% of the time, while 4% of the people went without an attorney had their DUI case dismissed or dropped.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

20% of DUI cases in Judge Pierce's courtroom are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving.
People who hired a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 26% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced only 12% of the time. 6% of people who went without an attorney had their DUI case reduced to Reckless Driving.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

Over the past five years 269 people represented themselves in a DUI case in Judge Pierce's courtroom, and they were conviced at a much higher rate than people with a private attorney or the public defender.
