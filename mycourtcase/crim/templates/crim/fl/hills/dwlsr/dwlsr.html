Driving While License Suspended Cases in Hillsborough County

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

48% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge cases are convicted. 28% of these cases are dismissed, and 24% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Driving While License Suspended cases?

Only 16% of people charged with Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge hire a private attorney. 12% go with the Public Defender. 72% decide to handle their cases on their own without an attorney.

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Hiring a private attorney is an advantage in these cases. People who hire a private attorney are convicted of Driving While License Suspended With Knowledge 43% of the time, while people represented by the public defender are convicted 69% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own, also known as "Pro Se", are convicted 46% of the time.

Can I get my Driving While License Suspended case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney are able to get their cases dismissed or dropped 41% of the time, while people who have the public defender have their cases dismissed or dropped 22% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own without an attorney have their cases dropped 33% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 22% of the time, while people with the public defender receive a withhold of adjudication 11% of the time. People who handle their cases on their own without an attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 27% of the time.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

People with cases in front of Judge McNeil are convicted 75% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge Taylor are convicted only 39% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judge Farr have their case dismissed 44% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge McNeil hae their case dismissed only 20% of the time.

People with cases in front of Judge Myers receive a withhold of adjudication 34% of the time, while people with cases in front of Judge McNeil receive a withhold of adjudication only 5% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Driving While License Suspended?

The most common penalty for Driving While License Suspended with Knowledge is a $270 fine. Less than 1% of people receive probation for this charge.
