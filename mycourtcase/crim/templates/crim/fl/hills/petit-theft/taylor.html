Petit Theft Cases in Judge Taylor's Courtroom

39% of people charged with Petit Theft are convicted. 22% of these cases are dismissed, and 39% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Petit Theft cases in Judge Taylor's courtroom?

34% of people charged with Petit Theft hire a private attorney in Judge Taylor's courtroom. 55% go with the Public Defender. 11% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is an advantage in Judge Taylor's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 21% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 52% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") are convicted 37% of the time.

Across Hillsborough County people who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 19% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 53% of the time. People without an attorney are convicted 24% of the time.

Can I get my Petit Theft case dismissed or dropped?

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 52% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender receive a withhold of adjudication 30% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") receive a withhold of adjudication 46% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Petit Theft?

The most common penalty for Petit Theft is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $200-300.
