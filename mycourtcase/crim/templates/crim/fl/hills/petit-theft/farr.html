Petit Theft Cases in Judge Farr's Courtroom

50% of people charged with Petit Theft are convicted. 25% of these cases are dismissed, and 25% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Petit Theft cases in Judge Farr's courtroom?

Only 20% of people charged with Petit Theft hire a private attorney in Judge Farr's courtroom. 60% go with the Public Defender. 20% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is a clear advantage in Judge Farr's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 26% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 62% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") are convicted 38% of the time.

Across Hillsborough County people who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 19% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 53% of the time. People without an attorney are convicted 24% of the time.

Can I get my Petit Theft case dismissed or dropped?

In Judge Farr's courtroom, people who hire a private attorney have their case dismissed or dropped 38% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 18% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") have their case dismissed or dropped 34% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 35% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender receive a withhold of adjudication 20% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") receive a withhold of adjudication 27% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Petit Theft?

The most common penalty for Petit Theft is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $200-300.
