Petit Theft Cases in Judge Lefler's Courtroom

Know Your Case, Know Your Chances.

42% of people charged with Petit Theft are convicted. 28% of these cases are dismissed, and 30% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Petit Theft cases in Judge Lefler's courtroom?

Only 30% of people charged with Petit Theft hire a private attorney in Judge Lefler's courtroom. 54% go with the Public Defender. 16% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is an advantage in Judge Lefler's courtroom. People who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 22% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 56% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") are convicted 34% of the time.

Across Hillsborough County people who hire a private attorney are convicted of Petit Theft 19% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 53% of the time. People without an attorney are convicted 24% of the time.

Can I get my Petit Theft case dismissed or dropped?

In Judge Lefler's courtroom, people who hire a private attorney have their case dismissed or dropped 42% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 20% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") have their case dismissed or dropped 30% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?

People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 36% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender receive a withhold of adjudication 24% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") receive a withhold of adjudication 36% of the time.

What is the typical penalty for Petit Theft?

The most common penalty for Petit Theft is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $200-300.
