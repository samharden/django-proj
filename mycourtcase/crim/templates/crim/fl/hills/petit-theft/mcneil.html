Petit Theft Cases in Judge McNeil's Courtroom

47% of people charged with Petit Theft are convicted. 37% of these cases are dismissed, and 16% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Petit Theft cases in Judge McNeil's courtroom?

Only 8% of people charged with Petit Theft hire a private attorney in Judge McNeil's courtroom. 88% go with the Public Defender. 4% decide to handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

Having a private attorney is an advantage in Judge McNeil's courtroom. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") are convicted 21% of the time.

What about getting a Withhold of Adjudication?
