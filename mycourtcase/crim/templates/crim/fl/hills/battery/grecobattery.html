Battery Cases in Judge Greco's Courtroom

59% of people charged with Misdemeanor Battery in Judge Greco's courtroom have their cases dismissed. 25% of people receive a conviction, and 16% receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Battery cases in Judge Greco's courtroom?

25% of people accused of Battery hire a private attorney in Judge Greco's courtroom. 59% use the public defender. 16% of people go without an attorney and handle their cases on their own, which is called "Pro Se."

Having a private attorney is a big advantage in Judge Greco's courtroom. The typical penalty for battery is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $600-700.
