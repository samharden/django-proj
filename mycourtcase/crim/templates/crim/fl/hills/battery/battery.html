Battery Cases in Hillsborough County

60% of people charged with Misdemeanor Battery in Hillsborough County have their cases dismissed. 23% of people are convicted, and 17% of people receive a withhold of adjudication, meaning that while they may receive a penalty for the crime, they do not receive a conviction on their record.

Do people hire an attorney for Battery cases?

21% of people accused of Battery hire a private attorney. The majority, 67%, use the public defender. Only 12% of people go without an attorney and handle their cases on their own, which is called “Pro Se.”

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

People who hire a private attorney were convicted of Misdemeanor Battery 11% of the time, while people who use the public defender are convicted 29% of the time. People without an attorney are convicted 12% of the time.

Can I get my Battery case dismissed or dropped?

People who hire a private attorney have their case dismissed or dropped 63% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender have their case dismissed or dropped 59% of the time, and people who go without an attorney (“Pro Se”) have their case dismissed or dropped 62% of the time.

What about a withhold of adjudication?

Getting a withhold of adjudication means that you do not receive a conviction on your record, even though you may receive a penalty for the crime. This can be very important in applying for emloyment or on a background check. People who hire a private attorney receive a withhold of adjudication 26% of the time, while people who use the Public Defender receive a withhold of adjudication 12% of the time. People who go without an attorney (“Pro Se”) receive a withhold of adjudication 26% of the time.

Does what judge I have make a difference?

Judge Myers issues more battery convictions than any other Hillsborough County judge, while Judge Jeske issues the fewest.

Judge McNeil dismisses more battery cases than any other Hillsborough County judge, while Judge Myers dismisses the fewest.

Judge Taylor issues more withholds of adjudication in battery cases than any other Hillsborough County judge, while Judge McNeil issues the fewest.

What is the typical penalty for battery?

The typical penalty for battery is 6 months probation and a fine in the amount of $600-700.
