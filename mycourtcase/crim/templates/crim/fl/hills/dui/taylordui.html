DUI Cases in Judge Taylor's Courtroom

In Judge Taylor's courtroom 54% of people accused of a DUI are convicted. 34% have their case reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving, and only 12% of DUI cases are dismissed.

Do people hire an attorney for DUI cases in Judge Taylor's courtroom?

Nearly everyone in Judge Taylor's courtroom who is accused of DUI has an attorney for their case. The majority of people (69%) hire a private attorney, while about a quarter (24%) use the public defender. Only 7% of people did not hire an attorney, which is called "Pro Se."

Does hiring a private attorney make a difference?

People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 35% of the time. People who go without an attorney, what lawyers call "Pro Se," avoid a DUI conviction 37% of the time.

The average for judges in Hillsborough County is slightly higher for private attorneys, and slightly lower for those represented by the public defender. People who hire a private attorney are able to avoid a DUI conviction 51% of the time, while people who use the public defender only avoid a DUI conviction 40% of the time. People who go without an attorney avoid a DUI conviction only 26% of the time.

Why is avoiding a DUI conviction so important?

DUI convictions not only carry stiff penalties, they can have lasting economic consequences for both your insurance and employment. If you are convicted of a DUI, your car insurance rates will increase and can add up to thousands of dollars over time. If you hold a professional license a DUI conviction can put your ability to work in jeopardy.

Can I get my DUI case dismissed or dropped?

Only 10% of the total DUI cases in Judge Taylor's courtroom are dismissed or dropped. People who hire a private attorney have their case dropped or dismissed only 9% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case dropped or dismissed 16% of the time. People who go without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case dismissed or dropped 17% of the time.

What about DUI cases reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving?

34% of DUI cases in Judge Taylor's court room are reduced to the lesser charge of Reckless Driving. People who hire a private attorney have their case reduced to Reckless Driving 41% of the time, while people represented by the Public Defender have their case reduced to Reckless Driving only 18% of the time. People who went without an attorney ("Pro Se") get their case reduced to Reckless Driving 22% of the time.

Shouldn't I just represent myself?

7% of people accused of DUI represent themselves in Judge Taylor's courtroom These people, on average, are far more likely to receive a DUI conviction than people who hire private attorneys.

People who go without an attorney are convicted of a DUI charge 63% of the time, while those with the public defender are convicted 65% of the time. People who hire a private attorney are convicted 49% of the time.
